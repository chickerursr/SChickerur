About

Where Computation Meets
Clinical Reality

I am a researcher, trainer, mentor, consultant, Professor of Computer Science and Engineering at KLE Technological University, and Director of the Centre for Artificial Intelligence Research (CAIR).

My research bridges hardware-level GPU/QPU acceleration with clinical utility — developing high-fidelity AI diagnostic systems for ophthalmology, radiology, and medical imaging informatics. I specifically focus on deploying distributed deep learning frameworks to solve complex medical imaging challenges such as automated grading systems for diabetic retinopathy.

I am an NVIDIA DLI Certified Instructor and Ambassador, former Execom Member of the IEEE Signal Processing Society (Bangalore Chapter, 2007–2009), and a member of the IEEE P3120 Standard for Quantum Computing Architecture committee.

I served as one of the judges and problem setters for the Asia Regionals' ACM ICPC programming contest in 2007 and 2008, and was an Intel-IISC-VTU Multi-core Curriculum Development Committee member.
